# MuseRock Creative Loop Guide

## Overview

The **Creative Loop** is MuseRock's core framework for structured creative work. It guides you through four distinct stages, each designed to foster different modes of thinking and productivity.

```
Prime → Cloister → Divergence → Reflection
  ↑                                        │
  └────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Stages

### 1. Prime (The Compass)

**Purpose**: Define your creative intent, constraints, and references before you begin writing.

**Key Features**:
- **Intent Statement**: What are you trying to create?
- **Constraints**: Boundaries, rules, or limitations to guide your work
- **References**: Links, notes, or resources for inspiration
- **Session Context**: How this session connects to previous work

**When to Use Prime**:
- Starting a new project
- Resuming work after a break
- Refocusing when feeling lost or stuck

**Example Workflow**:
1. Write a brief intent statement
2. Add 2-3 constraints (e.g., "Keep chapters under 2000 words")
3. Reference previous material or research
4. Click "Enter The Cloister" to begin

---

### 2. The Cloister (The Pen)

**Purpose**: Deep, distraction-free writing. The AI stays silent until you explicitly ask for help.

**Key Features**:
- **Serif-First Typography**: Optimized for long-form reading and writing
- **Minimalist Interface**: No distractions, just your text
- **Keyboard-First Navigation**: Focus on your work, not the UI
- **Auto-Save**: Never lose your progress

**When to Use The Cloister**:
- Deep concentration mode
- Drafting or writing
- Editing and revising

**Keyboard Shortcuts**:
- `⌘/Ctrl + ,`: Open configuration
- `⌘/Ctrl + S`: Manual save
- `⌘/Ctrl + E`: Export menu

---

### 3. Divergence (The Spark)

**Purpose**: Explore contrasting ideas. The AI generates divergent directions, not generic suggestions.

**Key Features**:
- **Contrastive Idea Cards**: Multiple perspectives on your current text
- **Categorization**: Conflict, symbolic, structural, character, worldview
- **Keep/Discard**: Quick decision-making on what resonates
- **Read More/Less**: Detailed exploration of promising ideas

**How It Works**:
1. Select text in the editor or write a prompt
2. Click "Generate Divergent Ideas"
3. Review the generated idea cards
4. Keep what inspires you, discard what doesn't
5. Use selected cards to inform your next writing phase

**Idea Card Categories**:
- **Conflict**: Introduce or escalate tension
- **Symbolic**: Add metaphor or deeper meaning
- **Structural**: Reorganize or restructure
- **Character**: Develop or reveal character
- **Worldview**: Expand setting or context

---

### 4. Reflection (The Book)

**Purpose**: Close your session with intention. Log what progressed, what was abandoned, and where to pick up next.

**Key Features**:
- **Progress Log**: What you accomplished in this session
- **Abandoned Ideas**: What you tried but didn't keep
- **Next Steps**: Where to resume in your next session
- **History Review**: Look back on previous reflections

**Reflection Questions**:
1. **What did you progress today?** (Mandatory)
2. **What did you abandon or defer?** (Optional)
3. **Where do you pick up next time?** (Optional)

**When to Use Reflection**:
- End of a writing session
- After making significant progress
- Before taking a break

---

## Using the Creative Loop

### Basic Flow

1. **Prime** (5-10 minutes) - Set your intent
2. **Cloister** (15-45 minutes) - Deep writing
3. **Divergence** (5-15 minutes) - Generate ideas
4. **Reflection** (2-5 minutes) - Log your session
5. **Repeat** - Return to Prime for next session

### Flexible Usage

The Creative Loop is designed to be flexible. You can:
- Spend more time in one stage than another
- Skip stages that don't fit your current needs
- Loop back to earlier stages as needed
- Use Prime as a starting point for resuming work

### Session Tracking

Each stage entry is tracked in your history, showing:
- When you entered each stage
- How long you spent in each stage
- What content you worked on
- Your reflection notes (if any)

## Best Practices

### For Writers
- **Keep Prime brief** - 3-5 bullet points are enough
- **Use Cloister for focused bursts** - 25-45 minute Pomodoro-style sessions
- **Don't overthink Divergence** - The goal is quantity of ideas, not perfection
- **Always do Reflection** - Even 30 seconds helps with continuity

### For Teams
- Share Prime briefs with collaborators
- Use Divergence cards for brainstorming sessions
- Keep Reflection notes visible for handoffs

### For Solo Creators
- Build a routine around the loop
- Review your Reflection history weekly
- Use Prime to track project evolution over time

## Example Session

### Prime
```
Intent: Finish Chapter 3 - The Museum Heist

Constraints:
- Keep the chapter under 3000 words
- Include at least one plot twist
- Maintain tension throughout

References:
- Previous chapter notes
- Research on security systems
```

### Cloister
Deep writing session (45 minutes)

### Divergence
Generated idea cards:
1. **Conflict**: What if the security system was a decoy?
2. **Symbolic**: The stolen item represents lost innocence
3. **Character**: The museum curator is secretly the thief
4. **Structural**: End the chapter mid-action

### Reflection
```
Progressed:
- Drafted the first half of Chapter 3
- Established the museum setting

Abandoned:
- The side plot about the art collector
- Will revisit later

Next steps:
- Continue with the heist sequence
- Integrate one of the divergence ideas
```

## Troubleshooting

### Stuck in Prime?
- Start with just an intent statement
- You can add constraints later
- Remember, Prime is just a compass, not a prison

### Distracted in Cloister?
- Turn on Do Not Disturb
- Close other browser tabs
- Use fullscreen mode
- Set a timer for focused bursts

### No good Divergence ideas?
- Try providing more context in your prompt
- Select a specific section of text
- Adjust AI provider or model
- Don't be afraid to generate again

### Forget to do Reflection?
- It's never too late! Add a reflection later
- Next time, try doing Reflection before closing the app
